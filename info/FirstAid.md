# First Aid & Diagnostics Guide

Comprehensive guide to identifying and treating the most common issues in tropical houseplants.

---

## Nutrient Deficiencies

### Calcium (Ca)
- **Symptoms:** Distorted, curled new leaves with brown necrotic tips. Failure to unfurl.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Add Cal-Mag supplement or lime to the substrate. Check pH levels.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a new plant leaf failing to unfurl, showing curled, distorted edges and brown necrotic tips due to Calcium deficiency, 8k.

### Copper (Cu)
- **Symptoms:** Dark green but severely wilted, twisted, and deformed new growth.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Copper-containing micro-nutrient fertilizer.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of twisted and deformed new growth on a plant, showing deep green but severely wilted tissue due to Copper deficiency.

### Iron (Fe)
- **Symptoms:** Striking yellowing of new leaf tissue while veins remain stark, dark green.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Apply Chelated Iron. Often caused by high pH locking iron out.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Extreme macro photograph of a new leaf showing interveinal chlorosis: bright yellow tissue with stark, dark green veins, classic Iron deficiency symptoms.

### Magnesium (Mg)
- **Symptoms:** Interveinal yellowing (V-shape) while veins and edges remain green. Old leaves first.
- **Classification:** Mobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Apply Epsom salt (Magnesium Sulfate) solution.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of an older plant leaf showing a distinct V-shaped yellowing between the veins while the margins remain green, Magnesium deficiency, 8k.

### Manganese (Mn)
- **Symptoms:** Interveinal yellowing with numerous small brown necrotic spots.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Manganese sulfate spray or adjusted pH.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a leaf with light yellowing between veins and many small, scattered brown necrotic spots, Manganese deficiency detail.

### Nitrogen (N)
- **Symptoms:** Severe, uniform yellowing (chlorosis) starting on the oldest bottom leaves. Growth stops.
- **Classification:** Mobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Apply balanced fertilizer or high-nitrogen booster.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Wide shot of a plant with older bottom leaves showing uniform pale yellowing (chlorosis) while the top remains green, Nitrogen deficiency, botanical study.

### Phosphorus (P)
- **Symptoms:** Abnormally dark green leaves with purple or reddish tints along edges/veins.
- **Classification:** Mobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Use phosphorus-rich fertilizer (Bone meal or Bloom booster).
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a dark green leaf with a distinct purple and reddish hue along the margins and veins, Phosphorus deficiency, 8k.

### Potassium (K)
- **Symptoms:** Yellow margins that turn into dark brown, scorched, burnt-looking edges.
- **Classification:** Mobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Apply potassium sulfate or seaweed-based fertilizer.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a leaf with crisp, dark brown, "burnt" edges and yellowing margins, Potassium deficiency symptoms, highly detailed.

### Sulfur (S)
- **Symptoms:** Uniform yellowing of new, upper leaves, including veins.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Balanced fertilizer or gypsum.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of the crown of a plant showing new leaves that are uniformly pale yellow, including the veins, Sulfur deficiency.

### Zinc (Zn)
- **Symptoms:** Small, bunched new rosettes with mottled yellow spots. Short internodes.
- **Classification:** Immobile Nutrient.
- **Treatment:** Zinc-containing micro-nutrient spray.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a plant's new growth showing stunted, bunched leaves in a rosette pattern with mottled yellow patches, Zinc deficiency.

## Pathogens & Pests

### Mealybugs
- **Signs:** White, fluffy cotton-like masses in leaf axils and crevices.
- **Treatment:** Dab with Isopropyl alcohol (70%) on a Q-tip. Use soil systemics for severe infestations.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Extreme macro photograph of a cluster of white, fluffy, cotton-like Mealybugs nestled in the crevice where a leaf meets the stem, 8k.

### Root Rot
- **Signs:** Mushy black stems, foul smell, rapid yellowing.
- **Protocol:** Unpot, cut away mushy roots, soak in 3% Hydrogen Peroxide, repot in airy mix.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Photograph of plant roots being inspected, showing dark, mushy, slimy black roots contrasted with healthy white ones, Root Rot diagnosis.

### Spider Mites
- **Signs:** Fine webbing, dusty/sandy yellow stippling on leaf surfaces.
- **Bio-Control:** Phytoseiulus persimilis mites.
- **Chemical:** Specific Acaricides. Note: High humidity deters mites.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Extreme macro photograph of the underside of a leaf covered in fine silken webbing and tiny pale yellow stippling dots, Spider Mite infestation.

### Thrips
- **Signs:** Silvery scraped patches, tiny black dots (feces).
- **Bio-Control:** Amblyseius cucumeris mites.
- **Chemical:** Spinosad or systemic sprays.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Macro photograph of a leaf surface showing characteristic silvery, scraped-looking patches and tiny black fecal specks, Thrips damage detail, 8k.
