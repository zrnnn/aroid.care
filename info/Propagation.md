# Propagation Architecture

Techniques for multiplying and expanding your botanical collection.

---

## 1. Propagation Techniques

### Division
Used for clustering plants (Alocasia corms, Calathea rhizomes, Strelitzia).
- **Method:** Remove plant from pot and gently pull apart root clusters or harvest corms.
- **Requirement:** Each division must have its own established root system or growth point.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* Professional photograph of a gardener's hands gently dividing an Alocasia root cluster, showing healthy corms and roots, clean workspace, soft lighting.

### Node & Stem Cuttings
The definitive method for climbing aroids like Monstera and Philodendron.
- **Identify the Node:** Locate the swollen bump where leaves and aerial roots emerge.
- **The Cut:** Use sterilized shears to cut 1-2 inches below the node.
- **Callousing:** Let the cut end dry for 30-60 minutes to prevent rot.
- **Incubation:** Place the node in a rooting medium with high humidity and bright indirect light.
- **Visual Description (Prompt):* High-resolution photograph showing a clean cut on a Monstera stem just below a prominent node with an aerial root, sterilized shears in frame, 8k.
